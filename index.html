03 Quantitative Modelling

Econometric Modelling: Agricultural Import Drivers

A quantitative research paper investigating why some firms make use of the preferential tariffs available under the UK-EU Trade and Cooperation Agreement while others do not. Based on 512 observations of 2022 UK import data and eight statistical model specifications.

  • Combined HMRC customs records and tariff data across 512 product-country combinations and built statistical models in Python to identify what drives firms to claim preferential tariffs
  • Used two specialist model types suited to proportional data, running eight versions of each to make sure the findings held up across different setups
  • Found that the size of potential tariff savings was the strongest driver of uptake, while origin rule complexity had little measurable effect once country and sector differences were accounted for
  • Written up as a formal research paper and presented to senior colleagues to inform trade policy thinking
