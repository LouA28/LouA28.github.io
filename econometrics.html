Econometric Modelling — Agricultural Import Drivers

A quantitative research project applying panel data econometrics to a large product-country dataset to identify the key statistical drivers of agricultural import volumes — from data wrangling through modelling, diagnostics, and visualised outputs.

Built with R, Python, ggplot2
Type Quantitative Research
Methods Panel data, Tobit, Logit, OLS
Data Product-country level, HS6

Overview

This project applies econometric modelling to understand what drives agricultural import volumes across a large cross-section of countries and product categories. Working at granular product-country level, the analysis identifies the relative importance of factors such as tariff rates, partner GDP, geographic distance, and trade agreement membership.

The project follows a complete quantitative workflow — from raw data cleaning and harmonisation, through model specification and estimation, to statistical diagnostics, robustness checks, and visualised coefficient outputs that can be presented to both technical and non-technical audiences.

Methodology

  • Panel data regression with fixed effects

    Country- and product-level fixed effects absorb unobserved heterogeneity, isolating the within-variation that identifies causal relationships rather than spurious cross-sectional correlations.

  • Tobit model for censored outcomes

    Where the outcome variable is bounded at zero — reflecting non-importing observations rather than true zeros — a Tobit specification correctly accounts for the censored distribution and avoids biased OLS estimates.

  • Fractional logit for bounded rates

    For outcome variables bounded between 0 and 1 — such as utilisation rates — a fractional logit model is used rather than OLS, correctly handling the distributional properties of proportional data.

  • Robustness checks and diagnostics

    Each specification is validated through residual analysis, heteroskedasticity tests, multicollinearity checks, and alternative specifications — ensuring findings are not sensitive to modelling choices.

Models used

Model When used Key advantage
OLS with FE Continuous, unbounded outcomes Simple, interpretable baseline with unobserved heterogeneity controlled
Tobit Outcomes censored at zero Correctly handles pile-up at zero without biasing coefficients
Fractional Logit Outcomes bounded 0–1 Respects distributional constraints; avoids impossible predictions
Pooled OLS Cross-sectional comparison Baseline for comparing within vs between variation

Visualising results

Coefficient plots were used to communicate model outputs clearly — showing point estimates and confidence intervals for each predictor in a format that makes the direction, magnitude, and uncertainty of effects immediately readable, even for non-technical audiences.

Key findings

The analysis found that tariff rates and geographic distance are significant negative predictors of import volumes, while partner GDP and free trade agreement membership are positive drivers. Non-tariff measure indices show a more nuanced relationship — acting as a barrier at high levels but showing limited effect at lower intensities.

Results were robust across alternative model specifications and subsamples, and the fixed effects estimates align with theoretical expectations from trade economics literature, providing confidence in the identification strategy.
